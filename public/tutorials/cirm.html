Lattice paths and the commuting scheme

Consider a $n\times n$ square grid, and a set of $n$ paths entering from the left external edges of the grid and exiting at the top, in a such a way that the $i^{\rm th}$ incoming path on the left (counted from the top) exits at the $i^{\rm th}$ location at the top (counted from the left):


To each such set of lattice paths $P$ we associate a (Boltzmann) weight defined as follows:

$\text{wt}(P)=\prod_{i,j=1}^n$ : $1-\alpha\beta$, : $\alpha x_ix_j^{-1}(1-\alpha\beta)$;
: $1-\alpha x_ix_j^{-1}$, : $\alpha\beta(1-\alpha x_ix_j^{-1})$, : $\beta^{1/2}x_i^{-1}(1-\alpha x_ix_j^{-1})$ , : $\beta^{1/2}x_j(1-\alpha x_ix_j^{-1})$;
: $1-\beta x_i^{-1}x_j$

Why should we care about such weighted paths?

The commuting scheme is the affine scheme \[ \mathcal C = \{ (A,B)\in \text{Mat}(n,\CC)^2:\ AB=BA \} \] $\mathcal C$ is known to be irreducible, of dimension $n(n+1)$, and is conjectured to be reduced.

The group $GL(2,\CC)\times GL(n,\CC)$ acts on $\text{Mat}(n,\CC)^2$ by \[ (f,g)\in GL(2,\CC)\times GL(n,\CC) : \begin{pmatrix}A\\B \end{pmatrix} \mapsto f \begin{pmatrix} gAg^{-1}\\ gBg^{-1} \end{pmatrix} \] and leaves $\mathcal C$ invariant.

Therefore, the coordinate ring of $\mathcal C$, i.e., the ring $R/I$ where $R=\CC[a_{11},\ldots,a_{nn},b_{11},\ldots,b_{nn}]$ and $I$ is the ideal generated by $AB-BA$, is a representation of $GL(2,\CC)\times GL(n,\CC)$; let $H$ be its Hilbert series (or character).

Explicitly, introducing formal variables $\alpha,\beta,x_1,\ldots,x_n$ corresponding to the Cartan torus $(\CC^{\times})^{2+n}$, \[ H = \frac{K}{\prod_{i,j=1}^n (1-\alpha x_i x_j^{-1})(1-\beta x_i x_j^{-1}) } \] where $K$ is a Laurent polynomial that is symmetric in the variables $\alpha,\beta$ and $x_1,\ldots,x_n$ separately.

Matrix Schubert Varieties and pipe dreams

Matrix Schubert varieties are closures in $\text{Mat}(n,\CC)$ of preimages of type $A$ Schubert varieties under $G=GL(n,\CC) \to G/B_+$.

Given a permutation $w\in\mathcal{S}_n$, define the affine variety $$ \bar X_w =\overline{B_- w B_+} \subset \text{Mat}(n,\mathbb{C}) $$ where $B_\pm$ are the Borel subgroups of invertible upper/lower triangular matrices, and we identify $w$ with (the transpose of) the corresponding permutation matrix.

In equations, $$\bar X_w= \left\{ M\in\text{Mat}(n,\mathbb{C})\,:\, \text{rank}\ M_{\text{NW of }(i,j)} \le \text{rank}\ w_{\text{NW of }(i,j)}\ i,j=1,\ldots,n \right\} $$ i.e. $\bar X_w=\text{Spec}(R/I_w)$ where their defining ideals $I_w$ are determinantal. In practice, only a subset of rank conditions is needed (essential conditions).

We can encode these equations as follows:

msv = w -> ( if n =!= #w then ( n = #w; R = ℚ[m_(1,1)..m_(n,n),MonomialOrder=>{Weights=>flatten table(n,n,(i,j)->(n-1)^2-i*j)}]; ); w = w - toList(n:1); w':=inversePermutation w; M = matrix table(n,n,(i,j)->m_(i+1,j+1)); trim sum flatten table(n,n,(i,j)-> if w_i<=j or w'_j<=i or (i<n-1 and w_(i+1)>j and w'_j>i+1) or (j<n-1 and w_i>j+1 and w'_(j+1)>i) then ideal(0_R) -- inessential conditions; ignore else minors(1+#select(i+1,x->w_x<=j),M^{0..i}_{0..j}) -- essential rank conditions ) );

We are interested in $\text{init}(I_w)$ for a certain monomial ordering which turn every determinant (minor) into its antidiagonal term. [Knutson Miller '05] have shown that the rank conditions above (minors) form a Gröbner basis, and that the corresponding degeneration of $\bar X_w$ is a Stanley–Reisner scheme, i.e., a reduced union of coordinate subspaces.

The remarkable fact is that the NW pipes in each pipe dream reproduce the permutation we started from!

In fact, we obtain this way every reduced pipe dream with connectivity $w$, where reduced means that no two pipes cross twice.

In particular the degree of $\bar X_w$ is the number of reduced pipe dreams with connectivity $w$: degree ex

More generally, one gets a formula for the class of $\bar X_w$ in $H^*_{B_-\times B_+}(\text{Mat}(n,\CC))\cong \ZZ[x_1,\ldots,x_n,y_1,\ldots,y_n]$: $$ \text{mdeg}\,\bar X_w = \sum_{\substack{\text{reduced pipe dreams}\\\text{with connectivity }w}}\ \prod_{(i,j):\text{ crossing}} (x_i-y_j) $$ which is nothing but the double Schubert polynomial indexed by $w$.

The necessary generalization

  • None of these constructions explain how the geometry knows about the connectivity of the pipe dreams.
  • Pipe dreams are secretly an exactly solvable model, a.k.a. a quantum integrable system. This is implicit in the work of [Fomin, Kirillov '96] and developed in [Z-J '09].
  • The corresponding integrable system (based on the nilHecke algebra) is degenerate. [Maulik Okounkov '12] emphasize the importance of symplectic varieties being the right set up for QIS, leading to nondegenerate models.
  • [Knutson Z-J] and [Maulik Okounkov] noticed that there are natural deformations of these varieties which avoid certain technicalities (cf the stable envelope construction). We're therefore looking for deformations of conormal (Matrix) Schubert varieties.

The lower-upper scheme

Introduce after [Knutson '05] the lower-upper scheme to be the affine scheme: $$ E := \left\{ (M,C) \in \text{Mat}(n,\CC)^2:\ M C\text{ lower triangular, } C M\text{ upper triangular}\right\} $$ $E$ is a complete intersection of dimension $n(n+1)$.

Note that the diagonals of $MC$ and $CM$ must be permutations of each other. In fact, it's not hard to show that the irreducible components $E_w$ of $E$ are indexed by permutations $w\in \mathcal{S}_n$: $$ E_w = \overline{\left\{ (M,C)\in E:\ (MC)_{ii}=(CM)_{w(i)\,w(i)}\text{ and all $(MC)_{ii}$ distinct}\right\}} $$

One has $$ E_w \subset \left\{ (M,C)\in E:\ (MC)_{ii}=(CM)_{w(i)\,w(i)},\ \text{rank}\ M_{NW} \le \text{rank}\ w_{NW},\ \text{rank}\ C^T_{SE} \le \text{rank}\ w_{SE}\right\} $$ where the last two conditions can be rewritten $M\in \bar X_w$, $C \in w_0 \bar X_{w^{-1}} w_0$. [Knutson '05] conjectures equality: sadly, this conjecture fails for $w=1324$. It is however true that $(M,C)\mapsto M$ projects $E_w$ to $\bar X_w$.

We now encode this as follows: -- lower-upper scheme k=n=3; wt = (i,j)->(i+j-n-1)^2; maxwt = max flatten table(k,n,(i,j)->wt(i+1,j+1)); R = ℚ[m_(1,1)..m_(k,n), c_(1,1)..c_(k,n), MonomialOrder=>{Weights=>flatten table(k,n,(i,j)->maxwt-wt(i+1,j+1))|flatten table(k,n,(i,j)->wt(i+1,j+1))}]; M = matrix table(k,n,(i,j)-> m_(i+1,j+1)); C = matrix table(n,k,(j,i)-> c_(i+1,j+1)); lowerPart = A -> matrix table(#entries A,#entries A,(i,j) -> if i>j then A_(i,j) else 0); upperPart = A -> matrix table(#entries A,#entries A,(i,j) -> if i<j then A_(i,j) else 0); I=trim ideal(upperPart (M*C),lowerPart (C*M)); -- lower upper scheme: MC lower tri, CM upper tri comps=decompose I; #comps

NB: the $(MC)_{ii}$ play the role of deformation parameters. As these tend to zero, components recombine à la Kazhdan—Lusztig.

Fluxes in pipe dreams

Define the flux through an edge of the square lattice to be

$ \Phi_e = \sum_{\substack{\text{tiles }(i,j) \\ \text{right of }e\text{ if }e\text{ vertical}\\ \text{below }e\text{ if }e\text{ horizontal}}} M_{ij} C_{ji} \qquad\qquad$ so, a sub-sum of $(MC)_{ii}$ or $(CM)_{jj}$
Fluxes propagate north-east; note the conservation equation:
$ \Phi_W + \Phi_S = \Phi_E + \Phi_N $
This is how the geometry knows about connectivity! Note in particular that in $E_w$, the fluxes exiting at the top are the $w$-permuted fluxes entering from the left.

We can now define the $F_P$:

$ F_P = \left\{\vphantom{\raisebox{2cm}{}} (M,C)\in\text{Mat}(n,\CC)^2:\right. $
$M_{ij}=0$if $P_{ij}=$
$C_{ji}=0$if $P_{ij}=$
$\Phi_e=\Phi_{e'}$if $e$ and $e'$ cross the same pipe
$\Phi_e=0$if $e$ is a blank edge
$\left. \vphantom{\raisebox{2cm}{}} \right\} $

One can think of the Gröbner degeneration as a box-by-box process: for example, one can revlex $M_{ij}$, lex $C_{ji}$ keeping $M_{ij}C_{ji}$ fixed, starting from the north west and going towards the south east. The degeneration is symplectic; in particular fluxes are not affected by it! As the degeneration progresses, the pipes progressively coalesce from their state of quantum superposition to actual paths on the lattice.

Applications

  • The $F_P$ being complete intersections, we obtain this way a formula for $\text{mdeg}\,E_w$: $$ \text{mdeg}\,E_w = \hbar^{-n}\sum_{\substack{\text{generic pipe dreams}\\\text{with connectivity }w}}\ \prod_{i,j=1}^n \begin{cases} x_i-y_j&\text{crossing etc}\\ \hbar-x_i+y_j&\text{blank}\\ \hbar&\text{else}\\ \end{cases} $$
  • We can then project either $(M,C)\mapsto M$ or $(M,C)\mapsto C$; this corresponds to taking the limit $\hbar\to\infty$ in two different ways. We recover this way reduced pipe dream and bumpless pipe dream [Lascoux '06?, Lam Lee Shimozono '18, Weigandt '18] formulae for $\text{mdeg}\, \bar X_w$.
